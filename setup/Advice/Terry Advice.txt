# 1) Vida store review notes

### B) Customer service section requirement

**Issue:** If you list customer service hours, you must also show **response time**.

**Advice:** Add a line like *“Response time: within X hours”* under customer service info (he pointed out you had this on the other store but needed it here too).

### C) Social media links (IMPORTANT)

**What you did:** Added FB / IG / Pinterest links.

**Advice:** For **new stores**, social links with low activity can be a **negative trust signal**.

- Remove social icons/links from store and from GMC for now.
- Build socials “behind the scenes” first.
- He suggested targets before linking:
    - About **50 posts** on each platform (FB/IG/Pinterest)
    - Around **100 followers** on each
- Then add socials later (he suggested around **3 months old** store for longevity/trust).

### D) Font size / readability

**Observation:** Text felt too large (headers + body).

**Advice:** Reduce by “a couple pixels” so it’s easier to read.

### E) Hyperlinks on policy pages

**Issue:** Some references weren’t clickable (emails, “contact us”, tracking, etc.).

**Advice:** Hyperlink everything:

- Make email clickable
- “Track your order” → actual tracking page
- “Contact us” → contact page
- Any internal page references → link them
    
    Google likes easy navigation.
    

### F) Terms/Policies formatting

**Issue:** Some pages felt too wide/left-aligned and hard to read.

**Advice:** Bring text width in / center it (make it easier to read).

### G) Logo pixelation at certain screen widths

**Issue you raised:** Logo looks pixelated when browser is resized (tablet widths).

**Diagnosis:** It’s switching between **mobile logo** and **desktop logo** depending on width.

**Advice:** Replace/fix the **mobile logo asset** (higher quality / correct dimensions).

### H) Product pages / content uniqueness

**Good news:** He said:

- Titles + descriptions appear unique
- Store layout is generally strong aside from font/spacing/social links

### I) Microsoft Clarity

**Advice:** Install **Microsoft Clarity** (Shopify app) ASAP.

- Helps identify if Google manual reviewers visit (often appears as “Google India”)

# 5) Your questions + his direct answers

### Q1) “Should we space out applying to GMC because both stores are under the same LLC?”

**Answer:** He didn’t say you must delay. No strict “limit” from Google, but he later advised not to stack too many stores under one LLC long term for risk management.

### Q2) “Should we wait 7 days and do shipping one day, returns the next, etc.?”

**Answer:** **No.** He called this “rubbish.”

His reasoning: Google doesn’t really start checking seriously until products are uploaded/processed.

### Q3) “When do we connect Google Ads to GMC?”

**Answer:** Connect once products are approved (green), otherwise the ads side won’t have products to run.

He strongly recommended: **PMAX > Search** for this model.

### Q4) “My Google Ads account got ‘circumventing systems’ before. Could I just make a new Ads account?”

**Answer:** **Yes.**

He said he has many accounts under the same company; a few get flagged but others remain fine.

Key is:

- New payment method (virtual cards like Revolut/Airwallex/Mercury)
- Clean proxy + clean anti-detect environment

### Q5) “Do proxies/anti-detect ever stop working?”

**Answer:** He thinks it’s unlikely short term because detection and evasion both evolve, but Google keeps tightening checks.

### Q6) “What can we do / not do while using proxies?”

**Answer (simplified):**

- It’s mostly about **warming** the proxy + having a believable browsing history/environment.
- Biggest mistake is creating accounts immediately on a fresh proxy.
- Warmup example he gave: browse common US sites (news, shopping, weather, sports), close, repeat next day.

### Q7) “Gmail creation is hard. What about QR code / US text verification?”

**Answer:** He acknowledged this is newer.

- You told him switching from desktop to mobile browser avoided it (worked).
- He warned against **temporary SMS numbers** you can’t reuse later (because you might need that number again).
- Rental/owned numbers are safer.

### Q8) “We’re using a ‘cracked’ Shrine theme zip. Is that an issue?”

**Answer:** *Potentially*, but he didn’t think it’s likely to cause issues if it’s a fresh install.

**Main warning:** Don’t copy themes/pages from store-to-store (it can cause a lot of problems).

### Q9) “If we get suspended, do we need a new domain and rebuild everything?”

**Answer:** Not immediately.

- You can try a **new GMC using the same domain**.
- If repeated failures, then consider changing domain.

**If making a new GMC:**

- Must delete the old GMC correctly (he referenced a community video)
- New GMC should have:
    - New IP / new proxy
    - New anti-detect profile (Dolphin browser env)
    - New address + phone
    - New Gmail
    - Slightly rewritten policies
        
        Only the **domain** can stay the same.
        

### Q10) “How many stores per LLC?”

**Answer:** No official limit, but he suggested **around 5** as a practical risk-management number (not a hard rule).

### Q11) “Any reference stores for font sizing and layout?”

**Answer:** No single template store.

- His suggestion: use **Google Shopping** and look at stores actively advertising, then mirror their general font sizing/layout (and confirm mobile looks good).
